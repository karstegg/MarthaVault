# DMR Instruction - Prevention of Shaft and Winder Accidents

**Document Reference:** CIOM-INSTR.NOV/2025
**Date Issued:** 01/12/2025
**Source:** Department of Mineral Resources and Petroleum Resources
**Enquiries:** C Dlamini

## Summary

Critical regulatory instruction issued by the DMR regarding prevention of shaft and winder accidents, effective immediately.

## Background

- **72 shaft and winder-related incidents** reported since January 1, 2024
- Increasing number of accidents industry-wide causing concern
- Recent fatal incidents: Impala 11 shaft (Nov 2023), Blyvoor Gold (Oct 2025)
- Multiple incidents of employees trapped underground due to shaft/winder failures

## Major Contributing Factors

- Objects falling in shaft
- Winder/conveyance running out of control
- Fracture/failure of rope/connection
- Fracture/failure of sheaves/shaft/bearings
- Conveyance/shaft/crosshead issues
- Derailments
- Conveyance leaving guides
- Fracture/failure of brakes
- Safety catches failures
- Overwind
- Depth indicator malfunctions

## Statutory Requirements

### 1. Risk Assessment
Conduct risk assessments on all shaft and winders to ensure compliance with legal provisions.

### 2. Design Compliance
Adhere to **Regulation 8.13 of the MHSA** for design, safe operation, and running of all winding plants.

### 3. Training (Compulsory)
- Engineers and artisans must be properly trained in shaft and winding plant design and controls
- Training must cover: Electrical, Mechanical, Electronics
- OEM specialized training required
- Only trained and competent artisans may perform maintenance

### 4. Appointments
- All persons working on shaft/winding plants must be appointed per MHSA
- Includes third parties and OEM representatives

### 5. Reporting
Timely report all accidents and dangerous occurrences to Principal Inspector of Mines (PIOM) per Chapter 23 MHSA.

## Enforcement

- Instruction takes effect **immediately**
- DMR will conduct focused inspections and audits
- Enhanced engineering governance required
- Strengthened risk controls needed
- Strict legal compliance mandatory
